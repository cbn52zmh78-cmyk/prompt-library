## What DAVID actually is (and why that's the advantage)
DAVID is a **human-communication research engine** — corpus-first study of how humanity communicates: etymology, speech/phonetics, and the non-verbal layer (gesture, expression, sign languages, writing systems). **Dead/endangered-language revival is the flagship application** of that broader study — attested texts, reconstructed pronunciation, provenance, *"prove what they said."* So the video product is **lip-synced avatar tutors that speak dead & endangered languages** (Latin, Ancient Greek, Gothic, Old Norse, Etruscan, Biblical Hebrew, PIE reconstructions…). Almost no one serves this — it's uncontested whitespace with three structural gifts:
- **Virality** — "hear a dead language spoken" is inherently shareable.
- **Credibility** — corpus-cited, attested, reconstruction-labeled (DAVID's existing rigor).
- **Defensibility** — competitors can't fake provenance; the corpus + attestation IS the moat.

**Same DNA as Stonebridge:** cited, attested, honesty-by-design. Clean sister-brand fit under the parent.

## Market reframe
Don't fight the $85B mass-language market head-on. DAVID's defensible niche = **classical / dead / endangered / heritage languages + academia + culture.** Smaller TAM, near-zero competition, built-in virality, and institutional buyers (universities, classics/seminary programs, museums, heritage & revival communities). The scan's tailwinds still apply (AI-ed ~26% CAGR, AI avatars ~30%), and its wedge #1 (rare languages + lip-sync) is DAVID's *entire home turf*.

## The three monetization angles — reframed + sequenced

**1. CONTENT / AUDIENCE — lead here (capital-light, uses your existing Grok sub).**
Dead-language showcase + micro-lesson videos (attested texts spoken by lip-synced avatars, reconstructed pronunciation labeled). YouTube/TikTok → audience (high curiosity reach) → funnel. Proves the format and builds the channel + corpus asset cheaply.

**2. APP / PLATFORM — second.**
Learn classical/dead languages with avatar video + corpus-grounded lessons. Real existing market: classics learners, seminary/Hebrew/Greek students, hobbyists, conlang/history fans. Subscription $10–30/mo (scan benchmarks).

**3. LICENSE DAVID — third.**
The corpus + Grok training packs + the dead-language video pipeline → universities, classics/seminary departments, museums, heritage/revival orgs, edtech. B2B/institutional (enterprise/per-minute, SCORM/LMS).

*Why content-first:* cheapest, leverages tooling you already pay for, and dead-language content's viral curiosity builds audience + authority fast — which then justifies the app and attracts licensing.

## Production pipeline
DAVID (registry → attested corpus → reconstructed pronunciation/IPA → script/training pack) → **Grok Imagine 1.5** (lip-synced avatar speaking the language, native audio) → QA against attestation → publish. The corpus-first step is the differentiator — others can't credibly voice a dead language with provenance.

## Guardrails (these ARE the brand)
- **Scholarly honesty:** label *attested* vs *reconstructed*; cite sources; never overclaim certainty on dead-language pronunciation. Show the provenance — it's the moat and the ethics.
- **Content-IP / licensing:** ancient texts are public domain, but the public APIs (Wikipedia/Wiktionary/Wikidata) are CC-BY-SA — honor attribution/share-alike obligations.
- **AI likeness/voice:** synthetic/owned avatars only; no real-person likeness without consent.
- **Cultural respect (endangered/indigenous):** revival of living-endangered tongues must *involve and credit* the communities, not extract from them.
