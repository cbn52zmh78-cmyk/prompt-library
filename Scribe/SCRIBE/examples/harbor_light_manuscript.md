# Harbor Light

Genre: literary fiction. Work-for-hire engagement — the client owns all rights;
chain of title is on file. Originality confirmed — written from scratch.

## Chapter 1 — The Tide Returns
Elena Marsh had not seen the harbor in twenty years. The fishing boats still
left before dawn, their lanterns swinging like slow stars over black water. She
stood on the quay where her father had taught her to read the weather in the
clouds, and she felt the old town pull at her the way the tide pulls at a moored
hull. The cottage on Gull Lane was hers now, left to her in a will she had never
expected. She had come to sell it. She was almost certain of that.

## Chapter 2 — Salt and Memory
Thomas Reed ran the chandlery on the corner, as his mother had before him. He
remembered Elena from school — the girl who drew maps of places that did not
exist. When she came in for rope and lamp oil he did not say so. They talked of
weather and prices, and underneath the talk ran twenty years of everything
unsaid. Elena left with her parcel and a feeling she could not name.

## Chapter 3 — The Light Keeps
By the end of the month the cottage was painted and the For Sale sign lay face
down in the shed. Elena had begun, without deciding to, to belong again. The
harbor light still turned through the dark each night, patient and exact, and she
found that she had been counting on it without knowing.
